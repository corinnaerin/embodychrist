Small Groups

Small groups are the main place for spiritual growth, fellowship, and service opportunities. We believe we are created with a desire and potential to become more like christ, but we aren't made to do that alone. We gather in smaller groups to study the Bible, pray with and for each other, and celebrate God in each other through fellowship. We look to these small groups to be the initial place where we care for each other's needs and reach out together to serve the surrounding world.

We encourage everyone to be involved in some type of small group. It's where life happens, it's where growth happens, it's where service happens. We realize that not everyone is at the same place in their journey, so we aren't picky about what type of group you're in, but we do hope you will get connected at a deeper level through a group.

There are many types of small groups we encourage:

  • LTG's (Life Transformation Groups) 3-4 people of the same sex who meet regularly for study, prayer and accountability.
  • Discipleship Groups are groups of 3-12 people who gather to study the Bible, a book, or a topic. They also encourage each other and offer care for one another.
  • Koinonia Groups are any size and focus primarily on enjoying a shared experience (something everyone enjoys: books, movies, an activity, sport, food, games, motors, hunting, scrapbooking, etc.)

So, what does "koinonia" mean anyway?

  1. Close association involving mutual interests and sharing: association, communion, fellowship, close relationship.
  2. Attitude of good will that manifests an interest in a close relationship: generosity, fellow-feeling, altruism.

Acts 2:42: "They devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching and to the fellowship (koinonia), to the breaking of bread and to prayer."
